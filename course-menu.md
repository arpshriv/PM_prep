# PM Interview Prep Course — Navigation

## Meta (Product Design PM)

### Study Path (recommended order)
1. **Product Sense: Motivation & Context** — why does this product exist? Mission, business, ecosystem
2. **Product Sense: Target Audience** — systematic segmentation, prioritization criteria
3. **Product Sense: Problem Identification** — break down, prioritize, thrive in ambiguity
4. **Product Sense: Solution Development** — creative, scalable, user-reaction-informed design
5. **Analytical Thinking: Product Rationale & Goals** — purpose → goals → subgoals
6. **Analytical Thinking: Metrics & Monitoring** — north star, input metrics, guardrails, time horizons
7. **Analytical Thinking: Trade-Offs** — data-driven decisions, 80% solutions, tough calls
8. **Mock Interview** — full Product Sense + Analytical Thinking simulation

### Scoring
Meta uses the actual interview rubric: **1-5 scale** per dimension
- 1 = Insufficient, 2 = Moderate, 3 = Solid, 4 = Strong, 5 = Exceptional
- To pass: need Solid (3) or above on all dimensions
- To get Strong Hire: need Strong (4) or Exceptional (5) on most dimensions

### Behavioral is NOT the soft round (read `behavioral/ai-era-behavioral.md`)
Frameworks (STAR/BLUF/SOAR) still hold — but what goes *inside* them is what's dated. The four lenses have shifted:
- **Conflict** → trust deficit: role blur, the "just build it" pattern, expertise discounting
- **Feedback** → real-time, model-in-the-loop (not 1:1 + process over a week)
- **Scrappy** → delegation judgment (which 80% goes to AI), not grind
- **XFN** → who *needed* to be in the room, not "I rallied everyone"

The move: open with a one-sentence meta-frame about how the *work* has changed, then your STAR/GRAIL body lands inside a frame that feels current.
