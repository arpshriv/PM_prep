# Canonical PM Interview Frameworks (Arpit's source-of-truth)

> The three frameworks to use in interviews: **Product Sense (Comprehend)**, **Analytical**, **Strategy**.
> These are Arpit's own refined versions — treat as canonical. Companion to `google/frameworks-l6-l7.md`
> (deeper "how to think" + L6/L7 altitude) and `examples/model-answers.md` (10/10 worked examples).

---

## 1. PRODUCT SENSE — "Comprehend" framework

### Comprehend: clarify goals & assumptions
- Understand the prompt, break it down
- **Why should we care?** Mission · strengths & weaknesses · latent behaviors · anything in the press? · will it meet business goals?
- **Macro trends:** RTO · rising interest rates · tariffs/inflation · AI boom · drone delivery · AR/VR

### Identify users
- Who are the key users/personas? Prioritize the top persona and say why; **select one**.
- Marketplace/Ecosystem → focus on one side and why
- Demographic / psychographic / behavioral segmentation

### Report needs: needs & challenges
- Marketplace/Ecosystem → focus on one
- User journey and pain points
- **Prioritize top 2-3 challenges** — why these? (TIP: prioritization matrix → pick pain points with most potential vs goals)

### Product principles
- Lean into strengths, minimize weaknesses
- Meet users where they are
- Focus on the **crux** of the problem (e.g., address anxiety, build discipline)

### Brainstorm solutions
- TIP if stuck — the **R's**: reversing relationships, repurposing resources, recombining elements

### Select MVP + success metrics
- Why this MVP? Why these metrics?
- TIP 1: prioritization matrix / ROI estimate → pick highest-potential solution
- TIP 2: metrics ← goals (key metrics); secondary metrics ← user journey (for troubleshooting)

### Summarize: end with **"Imagine…"**

**Practice:**
- Launching a new pair of AR glasses — how would you take it to market?
- PM at a startup that invented a smart weighing scale — design the app, launch it, 12–18 month plan?

## 2. ANALYTICAL framework

- **What is the product designed to do?**
- **Competitors**
- **Why is the company investing?** Mission · ecosystem impact · revenue
- **When do users derive value?**
- **North star metric**
- **Ecosystem impact metric**
- **Secondary metrics:** user journey, a metric on each step; identify a **volume-based secondary metric that's a lagging indicator**
- **Guardrail metrics:** quality · cannibalization
- **Does bias exist?** Under what conditions does it emerge? When it emerges, what shape does it take?

**Practice:**
- 3–5 metrics to measure a podcasting app?
- Exec dashboard for DoorDash leadership — 3–5 metrics on company health?
- Success metrics for YouTube's search results page?

## 3. STRATEGY framework

1. **Break the prompt** — define every key term; show deep understanding before answering; lead with an insight that demonstrates domain expertise.
2. **Macro trends** — AI/tech shifts · competitive landscape · latent user behaviors (e.g., declining attention spans) · press · demographics.
3. **Company strengths & weaknesses** — what does this company do well vs poorly, and how does that relate to the problem?
4. **Ecosystem / business model** — stakeholders (advertisers, creators, consumers, platform) · demand vs supply side · what levers move the core metric?
5. **Core metric** — pick ONE north star and justify; skip irrelevant segments.
6. **Solutions** — 2–3 (or 1 really strong); novel, insight-driven, not generic; tie each back to metric + strengths + macro trends; explicitly skip/address segments and say why.
7. **Risks & trade-offs** — what could go wrong; interactions with feasibility, quality, cost.

---

## Cross-framework reminders
- Everything ladders to the goal you set upfront.
- Segment by behavior, not demographics; only segment if the space needs cutting.
- Name where your metric could lie (bias) — proactively.
- Close Product Sense with a visceral **"Imagine…"**.
