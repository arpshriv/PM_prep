## Product Sense (Blank Slate Productization)

### Core Questions (These are UNLIKE standard PM interviews)
1. "OpenAI has invented an AI that allows humans to communicate with animals. How would you bring this to market?"
2. "You're the founder of a company that invented cognitive profiling technology. How would you productize it?"
3. "The R&D team created technology that allows you to understand people's perspectives through brain scanning. What would you build?"

### Additional Practice (Same format — novel tech → market)
4. "OpenAI has created an AI that can perfectly predict someone's emotional state from text alone. How would you productize this?"
5. "Your team has built an AI that can generate photorealistic video of any historical event from text descriptions. How do you bring this to market responsibly?"
6. "OpenAI has developed an AI that can learn and replicate any person's writing style with 99% accuracy. What do you build?"
7. "You've built an AI that can diagnose mechanical failures in any machine from audio alone. How do you productize it?"
8. "OpenAI has created a real-time AI translator that works for 200+ languages including sign language. How do you bring this to market?"

## Product Execution

### Core Questions
1. "You're the PM for a new HomePod-like device for engaging with ChatGPT in public spaces. How would you measure success?"
2. "You're launching an AirPods-type product. Set a goal and define success metrics for a product that hasn't launched yet."
3. "You're the PM for Operator. How would you evaluate success metrics — and would you launch it or not?"

### Additional Practice
4. "You're launching ChatGPT for enterprise teams. Define the metric framework for the first 90 days post-launch."
5. "OpenAI's API error rate spiked 3x this week but customer complaints only went up 1.5x. What's happening and what do you do?"
6. "You're the PM for ChatGPT plugins. Usage is growing but retention is flat. Diagnose and propose next steps."
7. "Define the success metrics for a new OpenAI safety feature that limits model outputs in sensitive domains."

## Cross-Functional Collaboration (Case-Study Format)

### Core Questions
1. "Imagine we're working on a new ChatGPT feature together. What would the first three steps be?"
2. "We're about to do a product kickoff for a new API capability. How would you go about tackling that with engineering, design, and research?"
3. "We're building a new safety feature together. Let's work on it — how do you approach this?"
4. "You need to align research, engineering, and safety teams on a launch timeline for a model update. Walk me through your approach."

### Additional Practice
5. "Research says the model needs 3 more months of training. Sales says customers need the feature NOW. You're in the middle. What do you do?"
6. "The safety team wants to add a content filter that will degrade the user experience for 15% of queries. How do you navigate this?"

## Essential GRAIL Stories to Prepare

Map each to a story type:

| # | Story Type | Example Opening |
|---|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | 0-to-1 with no clear direction | "I'll tell you about building [X] from scratch when the market didn't exist yet..." |
| 2 | Changed mind based on data | "I was convinced [X] was the right approach until the data showed..." |
| 3 | XFN conflict with research/ML | "Research wanted [X], product needed [Y], and here's how we found alignment..." |
| 4 | Rapid iteration under time pressure | "I reduced our launch timeline by [X]% by..." |
| 5 | Mission-connected decision | "I chose [harder path] because the user impact mattered more than..." |
| 6 | Failure and learning | "The biggest mistake I made was [X], and here's what I learned..." |
